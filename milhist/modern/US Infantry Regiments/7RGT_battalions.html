7th Infantry Regiment Battalions

Modern Era, United States, Infantry

This second-pass battalion page ties the Cottonbalers' battalion icons more directly to the parent regiment's long 3rd Infantry Division story. The local icon set includes the 1st, 2d, and 3d Battalions, while the better-documented modern battalion story is centered more clearly on the 2d and 3d.

1st Battalion

1st Battalion, 7th Infantry Regiment

The local icon set preserves a 1st Battalion, but the parent regiment page's clearest twentieth-century through-line is the regiment's long service with the 3rd Infantry Division rather than a distinct modern 1st Battalion narrative. For that reason the 1st Battalion remains the most tentative slot on this page.

It works best as a heritage placeholder that acknowledges the icon set without claiming the same documentary footing as the 2d and 3d Battalions.

2d Battalion

2d Battalion, 7th Infantry Regiment

The 2d Battalion is the clearest way to express the Cottonbalers' mature divisional identity. The parent regiment page presents the 7th Infantry as one of the classic regiments of the 3rd Infantry Division across World War I, World War II, Korea, and later mechanized service.

That makes the 2d Battalion entry the best place to frame the regiment's durable battlefield reputation inside one of the Army's most famous divisional lineages.

3d Battalion

3d Battalion, 7th Infantry Regiment

The 3d Battalion is the other strong modern carrier of the Cottonbalers' line. Its place on the page reflects the same divisional continuity: a regiment whose identity became inseparable from the 3rd Infantry Division and whose battalions carried that identity forward after the classic regimental era ended.

In practical terms, the 3d Battalion slot is where the regiment's later mechanized and post-Cold War identity reads most naturally within the battalion-page format.

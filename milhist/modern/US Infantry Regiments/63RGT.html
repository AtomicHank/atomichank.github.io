63rd Infantry Regiment

Modern Era, United States, Infantry

The 63rd Infantry Regiment was part of the larger structure of modern numbered infantry lineages maintained by the U.S. Army. It belongs to the institutional side of Army history, where many regimental names were preserved for wartime expansion or later specialized use.

Its lineage did not produce the same uninterrupted public combat identity as some famous older regiments, but it still reflects the Army's effort to retain organizational depth and historical continuity.

The 63rd Infantry is therefore best understood as one of the many lineages that supported the Army's flexibility in the world-war and Cold War eras.

4th Infantry Regiment Battalions

Modern Era, United States, Infantry

This subpage collects the battalion insignia associated with the modern 4th Infantry Regiment. The current public battalion listing and the local icon set both present the 1st, 2d, and 3d Battalions.

1st Battalion

1st Battalion, 4th Infantry Regiment

The 1st Battalion represents one of the principal modern carriers of the regiment's long line from Tippecanoe, Mexico, and the Civil War into the Cold War and post-Cold War Army. In the twentieth and twenty-first centuries battalions of the 4th Infantry increasingly served in separate assignments, especially in Europe and later in lighter modular formations, rather than as parts of one permanent regimental team.

2d Battalion

2d Battalion, 4th Infantry Regiment

The 2d Battalion carried the regiment's identity through the same long transition from frontier line regiment to modern modular battalion army. Its place in the modern story reflects the 4th Infantry's post-1945 service in Germany, NATO, and later expeditionary roles, preserving a lineage better known in earlier wars for service in Mexico, the Civil War, Alaska, and the Aleutians.

3d Battalion

3d Battalion, 4th Infantry Regiment

The 3d Battalion rounds out the regiment's publicly listed modern battalion set. It stands for the later battalion-era continuation of the 4th Infantry's history, when regimental lineage survived through dispersed battalions assigned to changing higher headquarters rather than through the old nineteenth-century model of one regiment campaigning intact.

Research note: The 4th Infantry Regiment's battalion blurbs are concise lineage sketches based on the parent regiment history and the current public CMH battalion listings checked on 24 March 2026.

9th Infantry Regiment Battalions

Modern Era, United States, Infantry

This subpage gathers the battalion insignia associated with the modern 9th Infantry Regiment, the Manchus. The public CMH battalion list currently documents the 1st and 2d Battalions, while the local image set also includes a 3d Battalion icon.

1st Battalion

1st Battalion, 9th Infantry Regiment

The 1st Battalion represents one of the principal modern carriers of the Manchus' long 2d Infantry Division heritage. Its significance lies in preserving the regiment's line through the battalion era after the 9th Infantry had already built a classic division-centered identity in World War I, World War II, and Korea.

2d Battalion

2d Battalion, 9th Infantry Regiment

The 2d Battalion likewise carried the regiment into the modern battalion-based force. Together with the 1st Battalion it preserved the 9th Infantry's continuity after the era when entire regiments moved as single formations, linking the Manchus' older 2d Division history to later service in Korea, Alaska, and post-Cold War operations.

3d Battalion

3d Battalion, 9th Infantry Regiment

The local icon set includes a 3d Battalion graphic, but the current public CMH listing for the 9th Infantry Regiment documents only the 1st and 2d Battalions. This section therefore serves as a heritage placeholder in the icon set rather than as a fully documented public battalion lineage summary.

Research note: The 1st and 2d Battalion blurbs follow the regiment's public history and current CMH battalion listing checked on 24 March 2026. The 3d Battalion note reflects the difference between the icon set and the public battalion list.

3rd Infantry Regiment Battalions

Modern Era, United States, Infantry

This subpage gathers the battalion insignia associated with the modern 3rd Infantry Regiment, The Old Guard. The local icon set currently includes the 1st, 2d, and 3d Battalions, while the public CMH battalion listings emphasize the 1st, 2d, and 4th Battalions.

1st Battalion

1st Battalion, 3rd Infantry Regiment

The 1st Battalion carries the most familiar modern face of the Old Guard. In the post-1948 Washington era it became one of the regiment's principal ceremonial and security battalions, supporting funeral honors at Arlington, presidential ceremonies, and the Military District of Washington mission while preserving the oldest active infantry lineage in the Army.

2d Battalion

2d Battalion, 3rd Infantry Regiment

The 2d Battalion reflects the regiment's operational side. It was activated for Vietnam in 1966 and served with the 199th Light Infantry Brigade in III Corps, then in later decades carried the Old Guard's lineage into modern field service as an operational battalion separate from the regiment's ceremonial battalions in Washington.

3d Battalion

3d Battalion, 3rd Infantry Regiment

This icon is preserved here as part of the local battalion artwork set, but the current public CMH index for the 3rd Infantry Regiment lists the 1st, 2d, and 4th Battalions rather than a 3d Battalion page. For that reason this section should be read as a heritage placeholder inside the icon set, not as a fully reconstructed current battalion lineage entry.

Research note: The 1st and 2d Battalion blurbs follow the regiment's public history and CMH battalion listings. The 3d Battalion note reflects a mismatch between the local icon set and the public CMH battalion list checked on 24 March 2026.

1st Battalion

1st Battalion, 12th Infantry Regiment

This entry now anchors the battalion page in the regiment's origin or defining early identity, giving the 1st Battalion slot a more specific historical frame than the first scaffold pass.

The 12th Infantry Regiment was constituted on 3 May 1861 as part of the wartime enlargement of the Regular Army. It fought in the Civil War and then continued on the long frontier service typical of Regular infantry units in the late nineteenth century. In the years that followed it also served in the Spanish-American War, the Philippine-American War, and the China Relief Expedition, building a record that stretched from the Shenandoah and the plains to the Pacific and East Asia.

2d Battalion

2d Battalion, 12th Infantry Regiment

The 2d Battalion entry uses the regiment's middle or operational arc to give the page a clearer sense of how the parent unit developed over time.

After 1945 the regiment remained associated with the 8th Infantry Division through occupation duty and the early Cold War. During the Vietnam era, however, the regiment's main combat service came through the 4th Infantry Division. The 1st Battalion, 12th Infantry deployed to Vietnam in 1966 as part of the 4th Infantry Division and fought in the Central Highlands, making the regiment one of the many older lineages whose twentieth-century history crossed from one long-time parent division to another.

3d Battalion

3d Battalion, 12th Infantry Regiment

The 3d Battalion entry now carries the regiment into its later or enduring modern identity, tightening the page around the way the lineage is remembered in the modern Army.

Later battalions of the regiment served in Germany and in the post-Cold War Army. The 2d Battalion also maintained the regiment's lineage in modern combined-arms formations. Across these changes, the 12th Infantry preserved a history that linked Civil War origins, frontier and imperial campaigning, the 8th Division in the world wars, and the 4th Infantry Division's combat in Vietnam.

Local regiment reference pages: 16th Infantry Regiment, 18th Infantry Regiment, 26th Infantry Regiment.

This section compresses the immediate post-Vietnam transition into a single OOB view. The 16th Infantry Regiment Association records 2-16 Infantry as reorganized as mechanized at Fort Riley in April 1970, while 1-16 Infantry also served as mechanized infantry in the Germany-based 3rd Brigade. The 26th Infantry lineage places 1-26 Infantry back in Germany with the forward brigade after Vietnam.

Late-1970s REFORGER structure. This board keeps the same overall Cold War framework but shifts to the end of the decade, when the division had settled into its mechanized NATO mission. 1-34 Armor is shown only here because it rejoined the division at Fort Riley on 1 August 1979.

This late-decade view uses the same Cold War framework but adds the battalions clearly tied to the division by the end of the 1970s. The 1-34 Armor lineage places that battalion at Fort Riley with the Big Red One from 1 August 1979, while the 1st Infantry Division's Cold War history and the 26th Infantry lineage confirm the continued Germany-based forward brigade with 1-26 Infantry, two armor battalions, 1-4 Cavalry, and a field artillery battalion.

Comments and historyThis is the single most important strategic aviation base in the western Arctic, located 92 km south of Murmansk. It is a major bomber airfield with wide runways and taxiways. It has been operated by OGA (Arctic Control Group) to serve as a staging base for intercontinental bombers. It was home to 924 MRAP that flew Tu-95MS, Tu-22M, and Tu-16. An attack presence is provided by 88 OMAPIB, flying 31 MiG-27 and 9 MiG-23 aircraft in the early 1990s. The 967 ODRAP flew reconnaissance with the Tu-16R. Olenegorsk is headquarters for 5 MRAD and has served as a temporary summer facility for the Siaulyay Tu-126 AWACS airplane. Satellite imagery from 2003 showed numerous Tu-22 aircraft. Olenya is the name given on CAICA charts.

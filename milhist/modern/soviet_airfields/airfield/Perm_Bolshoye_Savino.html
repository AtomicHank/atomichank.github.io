Comments and historyMixed use airfield with small number of fighter and bomber pads. Services medium-sized airliners, with 39 parking spots near terminal and cargo area. During Cold War operated MiG-25, Yak-25, Yak-28. Received MiG-31s in 1991. Length given as 2500 m on a 1998 Jeppesen. The 87th IAD (Fighter Air Division) that was born in 1952 in the new airfield Bolshoye-Savino. The 87th IAD (Fighter Air Division) was created in 1952 at the new airfield Bolshoye-Savino; construction was under personal control of Marshal Zhukov, who was exiled by Stalin after WW2 in Urals to take command of the Ural military district.

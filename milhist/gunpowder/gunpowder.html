Gunpowder Era (1500 to 1850)

The Gunpowder Era in military history, spanning roughly from the 14th to the 19th century, marked a revolutionary shift in warfare due to the widespread adoption of gunpowder-based weapons. Originating in China, gunpowder technology rapidly spread to Europe and the Middle East, transforming battlefield tactics, fortifications, and military organization. Cannons, muskets, and artillery replaced traditional bows, swords, and heavily armored knights, making warfare more reliant on firepower and less on hand-to-hand combat. This era saw the rise of professional armies, advancements in military engineering, and the decline of medieval feudal structures as nations adapted to new forms of warfare, culminating in the industrialization of warfare by the 19th century.

Classical Era (477AD to 1500AD)

The Classical era of military history, spanning roughly from 500 BCE to 1500 CE, is marked by the rise and fall of powerful civilizations such as Greece, Rome, Persia, and Carthage, whose military innovations shaped the development of warfare. During this period, key battles like the Greco-Persian Wars, the Punic Wars, and the campaigns of Alexander the Great exemplified the strategic and tactical advancements of the time. Armies relied heavily on infantry formations like the Greek phalanx and the Roman legion, while naval warfare also became increasingly sophisticated. This era witnessed the professionalization of armies, the use of advanced siege technologies, and the development of military doctrines that would influence later periods. The Classical era set the foundation for many principles of military strategy and organization that continue to resonate through modern history.

Battle of Herat (484)

Tag, Tag, Tag, Tag

The Hephthalite–Sasanian War of 484 was a military confrontation that took place in 484 between an invading force of the Sasanian Empire under the command of Peroz I and a smaller army of the Hephthalite Empire under the command of Khushnavaz. The battle was a catastrophic defeat for the Sasanian forces who were almost completely wiped out. Peroz, the Sassanid king, was killed in the action.

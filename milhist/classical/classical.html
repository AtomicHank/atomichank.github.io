Classical Era (477AD to 1500AD)

The classical and medieval era of military history, spanning from the collapse of the Western Roman Empire in 476 AD to the end of the 15th century, was defined by the rise of successor kingdoms, the spread of Islam, the power of Byzantium, feudal warfare, crusades, steppe invasions, and the emergence of gunpowder. Armies in this period ranged from armored cavalry and disciplined infantry to horse archers, crossbowmen, pike formations, and early cannon. Battles were shaped as much by dynastic politics, religion, and logistics as by tactics on the field. The conflicts below show how warfare evolved from late antiquity through the high and late Middle Ages.

The Battle of Dara (530)

Byzantine, Sasanian, Belisarius, Mesopotamia

One of the defining battles of the early Byzantine-Sasanian wars, Dara showcased Belisarius' ability to combine field fortifications, disciplined infantry, and cavalry reserves to blunt a larger Persian attack near the fortified city of Dara.

The Battle of Taginae (552)

Byzantine, Ostrogoth, Italy, Justinian

Fought during Justinian's Gothic War, Taginae broke Ostrogothic resistance in Italy when Narses used a carefully prepared defensive position and concentrated missile fire to destroy King Totila's army.

The Battle of Yarmuk (636)

Rashidun, Byzantine, Levant, Khalid ibn al-Walid

Yarmuk was the decisive clash that shattered Byzantine control of Syria, as mobile Rashidun forces under Khalid ibn al-Walid defeated a larger imperial army in a multi-day battle near the Yarmuk River.

The Battle of Tours (732)

Frankish, Umayyad, Carolingian, Gaul

Charles Martel's stand against an Umayyad raiding force near Tours became one of the most famous battles of early medieval Europe and helped strengthen Frankish authority in western Europe.

The Battle of Hastings (1066)

Norman, Anglo-Saxon, England, William the Conqueror

The most consequential battle in medieval English history, Hastings ended with William of Normandy defeating Harold Godwinson and opening the way for the Norman Conquest of England.

The Battle of Manzikert (1071)

Byzantine, Seljuk, Anatolia, Alp Arslan

Manzikert became a turning point in Near Eastern history when the Byzantine field army collapsed against the Seljuks, accelerating the loss of much of Anatolia.

The Battle of Hattin (1187)

Crusades, Ayyubid, Kingdom of Jerusalem, Saladin

Saladin trapped and destroyed the main field army of the Kingdom of Jerusalem at Hattin, clearing the way for the reconquest of Jerusalem and transforming the course of the Crusades.

The Battle of Las Navas de Tolosa (1212)

Reconquista, Castile, Almohad, Iberia

A coalition of Christian kingdoms broke Almohad power in Iberia at Las Navas de Tolosa, making it one of the decisive victories of the Reconquista.

The Battle of Agincourt (1415)

Hundred Years War, England, France, Longbow

Agincourt is remembered for Henry V's dramatic victory over a much larger French force, with mud, terrain, and massed English archery turning the battle into a disaster for the French nobility.

The Siege of Constantinople (1453)

Ottoman, Byzantine, Gunpowder, Mehmed II

The Ottoman capture of Constantinople ended the Byzantine Empire and demonstrated the growing power of siege artillery, making 1453 one of the great watershed years in military history.

Maps Overview

Maps are humanity's attempt to understand and represent our world. From ancient cave paintings to modern GPS systems, cartography combines art, science, and technology to help us navigate both physical and conceptual spaces.

Map Design

The art and science of creating effective maps. Learn about cartographic principles, visual hierarchy, color theory, and typography that make maps both beautiful and functional communication tools.

Land Navigation

Master the essential skills of navigation using map and compass. From basic orienteering to advanced terrain association, develop the confidence to navigate in any environment without technology.

Digital Maps

Explore the revolution in digital cartography. From GIS systems to online mapping platforms, discover how technology has transformed how we create, share, and interact with geographic information.

Cartography

The fundamental science of mapmaking. Understand projections, coordinate systems, scale, and the mathematical foundations that allow us to represent our round Earth on flat surfaces.

GPS Technology

The Global Positioning System has revolutionized navigation and location services. Learn how satellites, receivers, and positioning algorithms work together to provide precise location data.

Topographic Maps

Detailed representations of terrain features and elevation. Master the ability to read contour lines, understand relief, and visualize three-dimensional landscapes from two-dimensional maps.

Historical Maps

Maps as historical documents reveal how our understanding of the world has evolved. Explore how cartography reflects the knowledge, biases, and aspirations of different eras and cultures.
